# Competitive Landscape

## Where YFYT fits

| Product | What it does well | What it doesn't do |
|---|---|---|
| **Robinhood** | Frictionless execution, clean UI | No education, no explanation, gamifies badly |
| **Webull** | Real charts, paper trading, data | Overwhelming for beginners, no AI layer |
| **TradingView** | Best charts on the planet | No trading, steep learning curve, no pedagogy |
| **Trade Ideas (Holly AI)** | Powerful AI signals | Expensive ($118/mo+), zero education, pro-only |
| **Investopedia Simulator** | Good paper trading | Static, no AI, feels like homework |
| **Tickeron** | AI signals + community | Still tool-heavy, not beginner-first |
| **RockFlow (Bobby AI)** | Beginner-friendly AI commentary | Passive investing focus, not active trading education |

## The gap YFYT owns

**AI-explained, education-first, active trading co-pilot for beginners who want to learn by doing.**

- Not the fastest execution platform
- Not the most feature-rich charting tool
- The product that turns a confused beginner into a confident self-directed trader

## Key insight

Retention isn't built on being useful today — it's built on the user becoming better over time. If we do our job, users graduate. When they graduate, they tell friends who are where they were a year ago. Word-of-mouth is the only growth channel that matters.

## Long-term moat

Point 6 of the Wingman design: over time, Wingman notices patterns in your trading ("You tend to enter too early before confirmation"). The longer a user stays, the more personalised their Wingman becomes. ChatGPT can't do that without the trade history. The data flywheel is the defensibility.
